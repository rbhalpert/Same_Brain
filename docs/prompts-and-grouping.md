# Same Brain — Prompts and Grouping Rules

## Prompt design goals
Prompts should:
- be readable in 2–3 seconds
- be broadly relatable
- encourage instinctive answers
- produce natural answer overlap
- produce multiple likely clusters
- not depend on niche knowledge

## Good prompt examples
- Something you would hate to hear from your dentist
- A food that is annoying to eat in the car
- A bad animal to bring to a wedding
- Something that should never crunch
- A smell that can ruin your day
- A bad thing to forget on vacation
- An animal that feels rude
- Something people pretend to enjoy

## Bad prompts
Avoid prompts that are:
- too broad
- too abstract
- too knowledge-heavy
- too niche
- too open-ended

Examples:
- Name any movie
- Best historical figure
- Something meaningful in life

## Grouping philosophy
Conservative grouping is better than aggressive grouping.

Players should trust the grouping system.
If uncertain, keep answers separate.

## Normalize in MVP
- capitalization
- punctuation
- whitespace
- leading articles
- trivial singular/plural differences
- obvious typo variants where confidence is high

## Safe grouping examples
- spider / Spider / a spider / spiders
- taco / tacos
- oops / Oops

## Avoid grouping in MVP
- snake / python
- money / cash
- dog / puppy

## Future expansion
Ambiguous group voting can be added later, but not in MVP unless clearly needed.
